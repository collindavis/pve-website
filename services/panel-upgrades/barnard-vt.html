Signs Your Barnard Home Needs a Panel Upgrade

Breakers that trip frequently — If you're resetting breakers regularly, your panel may be overloaded.

Fuse box instead of circuit breakers — Fuse-based panels are a sign of very old infrastructure and an insurance liability in most states.

Panel feels warm or makes buzzing sounds — These are warning signs that should be addressed immediately.

You're adding significant new load — EV charger, hot tub, home addition, heat pump, or electric dryer all require dedicated circuits. If your panel is at capacity, an upgrade comes first.

Home is 40+ years old with original electrical — Many Barnard homes of that era were built with 60 or 100-amp service that doesn't meet today's needs.

What a Panel Upgrade Includes

We replace your existing panel with a new 200-amp main breaker panel, run new service entrance cable if needed, install all new breakers, and coordinate the utility disconnect and reconnect with your power company. We file permits through the Town of Barnard. Downtime is typically 4–6 hours for a standard upgrade.
